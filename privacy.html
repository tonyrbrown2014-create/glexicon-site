Privacy Policy
Zero-Knowledge architecture. On-premise data residency. Glexicon has no visibility into your institutional data — by design, not by policy.

The Sovereign Position

Glexicon operates a fundamentally different privacy model to conventional software providers. We do not merely promise not to misuse your data — our architecture makes it structurally impossible for us to access it in the first place.

This is not a policy position. It is an engineering reality. The Glexicon Protocol executes entirely within your infrastructure. Your data never leaves your perimeter. Glexicon receives nothing.

1. Zero-Knowledge Architecture

Core Privacy Principle
Glexicon has zero knowledge of your institutional data.
The Glexicon Monster Protocol generates metadata tokens locally on your satellite deployment nodes. The tokenisation process is mathematically one-directional — tokens cannot be reverse-engineered to recover the original data without access to your own master database. Glexicon does not possess, transmit, or receive copies of your source data, token outputs, or any derivative of your institutional records at any stage of Protocol operation.
—

No Phone-Home: The Protocol contains no telemetry, call-home, or data-harvesting components. Your satellite deployment Nodes operates in complete isolation from Glexicon's infrastructure during all tokenisation activity.

—

No Asset Visibility: Glexicon has zero visibility into the content, structure, or volume of your media archive, patient records, financial ledgers, or any other institutional data set processed by the Protocol.

—

Dashboard Statelessness: The HQ Dashboard reflects operational metrics from your deployed satellite nodes. No underlying data is transmitted. The Dashboard is a read-out, not a data pipe.

2. Data Residency & Regulatory Compliance

Institutional clients operating under data sovereignty frameworks — including GDPR (EU/UK), CCPA (California), HIPAA (US Healthcare), and sector-specific financial data regulations — will find the Glexicon architecture inherently compliant at the infrastructure level.

—

On-Premise Residency: All metadata tokens generated by the Protocol remain on your satellite deployment Nodes, at your approved site locations, within your jurisdiction. Glexicon does not transfer, replicate, or store your token data on any external infrastructure.

—

No Cross-Border Transfer: Because Glexicon receives no data from your Protocol deployment, there is no cross-border data transfer to declare, consent to, or contractually protect against.

—

Sovereignty by Architecture: Your data sovereignty is guaranteed by the Protocol's design — not by a data processing agreement that a future policy change could undermine. The architecture does not permit the transfer. No policy amendment can change that.

For compliance teams: The Glexicon Protocol does not constitute "data processing" on behalf of the client under GDPR Article 28, as Glexicon does not process, access, or store personal data. No Data Processing Agreement is required between Glexicon and the client institution for Protocol operation. We recommend confirming this with your Data Protection Officer prior to deployment.

3. This Website & Visitor Privacy

Glexicon operates this website on a strict do-not-need-to-know basis for all visitors.

—

No Accounts Required: No login, registration, or personal information submission is required to access any content on this site, including protocol specifications, pricing, or legal documentation.

—

No Behavioural Tracking: We do not deploy advertising cookies, tracking pixels, cross-site trackers, or behavioural profiling tools of any kind.

—

Server Logs: Standard temporary server logs (including IP addresses) may be generated by our hosting infrastructure solely for cybersecurity defence, DDoS mitigation, and uptime monitoring. These logs are not used for any commercial purpose and are not shared with third parties.

—

Contact: Any voluntary communication you initiate with Glexicon is handled privately and confidentially. We do not retain correspondence beyond what is operationally necessary.

4. Policy Updates

Should Glexicon introduce any mechanism that changes the data collection posture described in this policy — including optional contact forms — this policy will be updated in advance of that change taking effect, with the revision date clearly noted.

The Zero-Knowledge architecture described in Section 1 is a structural property of the Protocol and cannot be altered by policy update alone. Any change to Protocol architecture affecting data residency would constitute a new product version and would be communicated to all active licence holders as a material change.
